Enterprise Design

Transforming Cloud Infrastructure Management for Enterprise Teams

A leading cloud services provider needed to simplify complex infrastructure workflows for enterprise development teams managing hundreds of distributed systems. The existing platform created friction at every step, leading to decreased productivity and increased operational risk.

Goal: Design a unified control plane that empowers teams to deploy, monitor, and scale infrastructure with confidence—while maintaining enterprise-grade security and compliance standards.

Role Lead Product Designer
Timeline 6 months (Q2-Q3 2024)
Team 2 Designers, 5 Engineers, 1 PM
Platform Web Application (Desktop)
The Challenge

Complexity at Scale Was Costing Teams Days, Not Hours

Enterprise development teams were drowning in complexity. What should have been simple workflows—deploying applications, monitoring performance, managing permissions—required navigating fragmented tools, cryptic interfaces, and error-prone manual processes.

Fragmented Workflows

Teams juggled 7+ disconnected tools to complete a single deployment. Context-switching between systems created friction, errors, and wasted hours tracking down information across platforms.

Lack of Visibility

Critical infrastructure health metrics were buried in dense dashboards. Teams couldn't quickly identify issues or understand system dependencies, leading to prolonged outages and reactive firefighting.

Security & Compliance Friction

Implementing security controls and maintaining compliance standards required expert knowledge and manual audits. Non-security specialists struggled to configure systems correctly, creating organizational risk.

Steep Learning Curve

New team members required weeks of training to become productive. Complex, technical interfaces assumed deep infrastructure knowledge, excluding product-focused engineers and operators.

Why it mattered: These challenges directly impacted business velocity and competitiveness. Engineering teams spent 40% of their time fighting tooling instead of building products. The platform's complexity created a scaling bottleneck—as organizations grew, operational overhead compounded exponentially.

Our Approach

Systems Thinking Meets User-Centered Design

We approached this challenge with a consulting mindset—treating design as strategic infrastructure rather than visual polish. Our methodology balanced deep user research with rapid prototyping and cross-functional collaboration.

1

Discovery & Research

We conducted 25+ user interviews across roles (DevOps engineers, SREs, security teams, product managers) to map pain points, workflows, and mental models. Key insight: users weren't struggling with individual features—they were struggling with system architecture and information design.

  • Conducted contextual inquiry sessions observing real deployment workflows
  • Mapped existing tool ecosystem and identified redundant/conflicting patterns
  • Synthesized findings into journey maps highlighting critical friction points
2

Strategic Framing

Rather than treating symptoms, we reframed the problem: how might we design a system that scales with organizational complexity while becoming simpler to use? We prioritized workflows that would deliver 10x improvements, not incremental gains.

  • Defined design principles: "Progressive disclosure," "Intelligent defaults," "Contextual guidance"
  • Mapped high-leverage opportunities based on frequency × impact analysis
  • Created north-star vision prototypes to align stakeholders on ambition
3

Iterative Design & Prototyping

We designed the system through rapid cycles of prototyping and testing. Every major interaction pattern was validated with users before engineering investment. High-fidelity prototypes allowed us to test complex workflows end-to-end.

  • Created modular design system for consistent, scalable UI components
  • Built interactive prototypes to test multi-step workflows and information hierarchy
  • Ran weekly usability sessions with 5-8 users to validate design decisions
4

Cross-Functional Partnership

Design success required deep collaboration with engineering and product teams. We embedded in engineering standups, reviewed technical constraints, and co-created solutions that balanced user needs with system architecture.

  • Partnered with backend engineers to design API structures that enabled better UX
  • Collaborated with security team to make compliance features user-friendly
  • Worked with product to define phased rollout strategy minimizing disruption
The Solution

A Unified Platform Built for Clarity and Control

We transformed a fragmented collection of tools into a cohesive control plane. The new platform provides teams with end-to-end visibility, intelligent automation, and progressive complexity—revealing advanced features only when needed.

Progressive Disclosure

Simple tasks stay simple. Advanced features are accessible but don't clutter primary workflows.

Contextual Intelligence

The system understands user context and provides relevant guidance, warnings, and shortcuts.

Trust Through Transparency

Users see what's happening in real-time with clear system status and dependency visualization.

Designed for Scale

Patterns and components adapt intelligently whether managing 5 or 500 systems.

Key Takeaways

Lessons That Scale Beyond This Project

Design for Scale Requires Simplifying Complexity, Not Hiding It

The instinct in enterprise design is to expose all controls—assuming power users need maximum flexibility. But we learned that true power comes from intelligent systems that handle complexity behind the scenes. Progressive disclosure means simple tasks stay simple, while advanced capabilities remain accessible when needed.

Workflows Are Systems, Not Sequences

Early on, we treated deployments as linear steps. But users don't think linearly—they context-switch, recover from errors, and jump between related tasks. Designing workflows as interconnected systems (not rigid sequences) created resilience. Systems thinking unlocks flexibility that step-by-step flows can't provide.

Constraints Drive Innovation When Used Strategically

Technical constraints (API limitations, backend architecture) initially felt limiting. But by partnering deeply with engineering, we transformed constraints into design opportunities. For example, API latency led us to design optimistic UI updates and background processing—patterns that became product strengths.

The best enterprise design doesn't feel like enterprise software. It feels like someone anticipated exactly what you needed, precisely when you needed it. That's the bar.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how thoughtful design unlocks business value in complex enterprise ecosystems. By treating design as strategic infrastructure—not visual polish—we created a platform that scales with organizational complexity while becoming simpler to use.
